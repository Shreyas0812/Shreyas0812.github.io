About

Systems thinking, end to end

My background bridges algorithm research and production software engineering. These two things rarely coexist in a single profile, and that combination is what I bring to hard robotics problems.

Research direction

Why distributed coordination?

Centralized coordination is fragile — when communication drops or a node fails, the whole fleet stalls. My thesis asks: can we design coordination that degrades gracefully instead of catastrophically? That question drives my work on GCBBA, distributed MPC for quadrotors, and event-driven replanning under partial connectivity.

Research focus

Decentralized multi-agent systems

Task allocation, consensus-based coordination, and communication-aware planning, especially in cases where intermittent links, timing failures, and partial observability make textbook solutions break down.
