My background bridges algorithm research and production software engineering — two things that rarely coexist in a single profile. That combination is what I bring to hard robotics problems.

Research direction

Why distributed coordination?

Centralized coordination is fragile — when communication drops or a node fails, the whole fleet stalls. My thesis asks: can we design coordination that degrades gracefully instead of catastrophically? That question drives my work on GCBBA, on distributed MPC for quadrotors, and on event-driven replanning under partial connectivity.

Research interest

Decentralized multi-agent systems

Task allocation, consensus-based coordination, and communication-aware planning — especially where real-world constraints like intermittent links, timing failures, and partial observability make textbook solutions break down.
